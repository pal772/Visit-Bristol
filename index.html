About Bristol

Bristol is a vibrant, diverse and innovative city. It's located just 120 miles west of London and is the largest city in the south west of England. The city is situated on the river Frome and river Avon.

Its former city centre port is now a culture hub, the Harbourside, where the M Shed museum explres local social and industrial heratiage. The harbours 19th centery warehouses now contain restaurants, shops and cultural institutions such as contemporary art gallery The Arnolfini.

Top 5 places to visit in Bristol

SS Great Britian

The SS Great Britian is a museum ship and former passanger steamship, that was advanced for her time. She was the largest passenger ship in the world from 1845 to 1854. she was designed by Isambard kingdom Brunel, for the great western steamship company's transatlantic service between Bristol and New York City.

Clifton Suspension Bridge

The Clifton Suspension Bridge is one of Bristol's most recognisable structures. Designed by Isambard KIngdom Brunel, it makes a turning point in the history of engineering and has come to symbolise a city of original thinkers and independent spirt.

Bristol Harbour

Once a busy dock where saliors and merchants would trade goods and set sail for voyages of discovery. The harbour covers an area of 70 acres.
It's now an attractive, modern development filled with restaurants, bars, shops and hotels.

M Shed

M Shed is a museum all about Bristol. From prehistoric times to the present day, M shed tells the story of the city and its unique place in the world.
Its contains 3000m square of exhibition space in three large galleries, new education rooms, a working train shed, space for volunteers and a new events and conferencing facility.

Arnolfini Arts

Arnolfini is an international arts centre and gallery. It has a programme of contemorary art exhibitions, artist's performance, music and dance events, poetry and book readings, talks, lectures and cinema.
There is also a specialist art bookshop and cafe bar.
